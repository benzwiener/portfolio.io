Roboextinguishinator 5000

June 2022 - August 2022

Motivation & Objectives

As commonly known, unexpected fires can cause extensive damage and even cost lives. But while modern day solutions like sprinklers and traditional extinguishers can mitigate fire damage, they too can lead to other damages and are not always able to provide an immediate response. For these reasons, we introduce the RoboExtinguishinator 5000, to automatically sense, aim, and eject a stream of water to quell any alarming heat signature within its effective range.

Youtube Video

Contributions

  • Developed supporting components in CAD
  • Designed the water ejection system
  • Commissioned both a thermal and camera sensor
  • Implemented sensor(s) and aiming function in Arduino

Outcomes

  • Derived inverse kinematics via Product of Exponentials to control the robot's movement
  • Developed four operating states for the robot including Sentry, Detect, Aim, and Extinguish programmed in Arduino
  • Repeatedly proved its effectiveness in testing with signatures placed at different distances and locations, without needing to re-initialize
