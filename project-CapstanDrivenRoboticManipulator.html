Capstan Driven Robotic Manipulator

December 2025 - Present

Motivation & Objectives

Cable, or Capstan, drives are a special reducer which implement cables and rigid links to transmit motion and torque for the desired application. Typical advantages of these systems are high stiffness, low backlash, low inertia, and simple design lending themselves to be a compelling option for robotic actuators. In the interest of gaining hand-on experience with Capstans, this project was undertaken to better understand which features are most critical and how design requirements may impact its configuration.

Goals

  • Transmit motion and torque to the output drum.
  • Achieve the sought 10:1 reduction.
  • Implement Capstan reducers to create a symmetric 5-bar linkage.
  • Demonstrate precise movement by spelling out a word/phrase.

Outcomes

  • Successfully built and operated an experimental Capstan test stand.
  • Derived Forward and Inverse Kineamtics
  • Created a successfull homing sequence to find the Capstan's range of motion with closed loop control.
  • Performed torque testing showing 30-50% efficiency
  • Constructed an operable symmetric 5-bar linakge/robotic manipualtor
