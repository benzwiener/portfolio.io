Inverted Pendulum

August 2023 - December 2023

Motivation & Objectives

When studying control systems and dynamics, inverted pendulums are a classic non-linear problem used to increased a student's understanding. Often they are made with a pendulum attached to a cart and driven via a pulley and motor to change its postion and thus maintain balance. For this project we were tasked to design, model, build, and impelemnt the physical inverted pendulum system and analyze possible control strategies.

Contributions

  • Supported analytical derivation with free body diagrams and linearized equations of motion converted to transfer functions
  • Designed CAD and 3D printed components
  • Created root locus plots to inform approriate changes for improving the transient response
  • Assisted in mechatronic setup

Outcomes

  • Analytically modeled the pendulum system via free body body diagrams and a linearized system of equations based on the system's transfer functions
  • Designed and built the Pendulum system
  • Implemented a PID controller to balance the pendulum upright
  • Extra Credit: Showed the system can maintain and return to its position while experiencing disturbances
  • Extra Credit: Proved the system can swing itself upright and maintain balance
