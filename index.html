A little about me

I am a mechanical engineer recently graduated from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln specializing in robotics/mechatronics and mechanisms. My thesis research focused on a novel variable stiffness mechanisms for industrial robotics (thesis), with publications at ASME IDETC conferences and a pending U.S. patent. I also led the project team and the design of a Remote Ultrasound Robot seeking to expand access of specialized healthcare in remote communities. I have hands-on experience in CAD design, FEA simulation, mechatronic prototyping, and manufacturing. At my recent internship with Relativity Space I desiged a modular gearbox from scratch and performed both hand calcualtions and FEA analysis to verify its structural integrity. Finally, I was able to build, manufacture, and test the modular device of its functionaity and provide critical data for informing future design decisions.
