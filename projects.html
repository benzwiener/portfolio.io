Highlighted Projects

Below are a few of the engineering and robotics projects that reflect my work and interests. Projects labeled "I" and "G" reflect individual or group efforts, respectively.

Capstan Driven Robotic Manipulator (I)

Capstan drives are a special type of reducer offering low inertia, high stiffness, and high precision. This project examines the design and utility of capstan reducers in robotic applications and demonstrates them in a 5‑bar manipulator.

Skills: SolidWorks, 3D printing, Arduino, MATLAB, wiring & soldering

Cartesian Variable Stiffness Mechanism (I)

Compact compliant mechanism designed to reduce energy use and wear in industrial robots by providing adjustable stiffness. The prototype demonstrated full 3D translation and a tunable stiffness from compliant to rigid for pick‑and‑place tasks.

Skills: CNC mill, manual lathe, 3D printing, Arduino, MATLAB

Remote Ultrasound Robot (G)

Teleoperated robot that sweeps and positions an ultrasound probe, aimed at expanding access to specialized imaging in remote communities.

Skills: Mechanism design, mechatronics, control, system integration

Inverted Pendulum (G)

Team project building and commissioning an inverted pendulum on a cart. The system balances, maintains its center position, and swings itself up into the upright configuration.

Skills: Control design, embedded implementation, mechanical integration

Robot Extinguishinator 5000 (G)

Five‑DOF robot programmed to autonomously locate and extinguish small fires.

Skills: Motion planning, embedded programming, sensor integration

Rubik's Cube Solver (I)

The Rubik's Cube is an iconic three-dimensional puzzle invented in 1974 but became well-known within pop culture during the 1980s. While the inventor Erno Rubik intended its use to teach students about building 3D objects, he quickly discovered its difficulty, taking him nearly a month to solve it. After learning how to solve a Rubik's Cube and seeing others build automated mechanisms capable of completing the various algorithms, this project set out to build a similar machine. While the project was not fully completed, it provided several valuable lessons outlined below and remains a candidate for future continuation.
