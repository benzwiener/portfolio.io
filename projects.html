Selected projects

Below are a few of the engineering and robotics projects that reflect my work and interests. Projects labeled "I" and "G" reflect individual or group efforts, respectively.

Capstan Driven Robotic Manipulator (I)

Capstan Drives are a special type of reducer offering low inertia, high stiffness, and high precison. This project examines the design and utility of Capstan reducers in robotic applications. To test its efficacy, an experimental setup was made and the Capstans were implemented in a robotic 5-Bar manipulator.

Skills: SolidWorks, 3D Printing, Arduino, MATLAB, Wiring & Soldering,

Cartesian Varaiable Stiffness Mechanism (I)

In this work, a design for a compact, compliant, variable stiffness mechanism was sought to provide a supplemental solution to save energy and reduce wear for industrial robots. Inital research investigated various designs and stiffness profiles. Building the first prototype, it successfully translated in every direction and changed its stiffness from low to rigid. Additional testing also demonstrted its ability to aid in other pick-and-place tasks like accomodating a robot's misaligned trjectory for inserting a bolt or dowel into a hole or other desired feature.

Skills: CNC Mill, Manual Lathe, 3D Printing, Arduino, MATLAB

Remote Ultrasound Robot (G)

A robot designed to be teleoperated sweeping through an ultrasound probe, aimed at expanding access to specialized imaging in remote or underserved communities.

Inverted Pendulum (G)

In this project, our team successfully built and commisioned an inverted pendulum. The pendulum was composed of a cart rolling along an aluminum extrusion rail and driven via a belt and motor. During testing we proved our pendulum was able to balance, maintain its center position on the rail, and swin itself into the upright balancing position.
