Rubik's Cube Solver

June 2022 - August 2022

Motivation & Objectives

The Rubik's Cube is an iconic three-dimensional puzzle invented in 1974 but became well-known within pop culture during the 1980s. While the inventor Erno Rubik intended its use to teach students about building 3D objects, he quickly discovered its difficulty, taking him nearly a month to solve it. After learning how to solve a Rubik's Cube and seeing others build automated mechanisms capable of completing the various algorithms, this project set out to build a similar machine. While the project was not fully completed, it provided several valuable lessons outlined below and remains a candidate for future continuation.

What I did . . .

  • Developed a program that checks hundreds of possible Rubik's Cube combinations and, based on camera sensor data, determines the next actuation
  • Built a rough prototype to determine optimal camera positions and design improvements
  • 3D printed most linkages and parts
  • Planned a final design using 6 stepper motors attached to the cubeâ€™s center pieces via 3D-printed fixtures

What I learned . . .

  • How to better assess the work required to complete larger projects
  • Improved coding skills and the importance of writing internal comments in code
  • How camera sensors work and how to interface with the Pixy camera sensor
  • The value of creating flow/coding diagrams before implementation
  • That it is okay to build on existing work (using published algorithms would have saved significant time)
